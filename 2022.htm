2022 CLEARINGHOUSE

Approximately 380,000 to 442,000 people are detained every year. There is a reasonable likelihood of increased funding for detention centers and increased funding for immigration enforcement officers in the coming years, which will result in more asylum seekers and long time U.S. residents held in remote detention centers. Currently, even non-detainee hearings are set for as far out as 2022.

 
 
 
 
 
  • Fundraising to pay immigration bonds
     
  • Storytelling featuring those detained and the communities impacted
     
  • Social media that organizes community power by connecting people across the U.S. experiencing the same injustices
 
 
 
 

GOALS

  • Create a bond fund that communities can contribute to and draw from to post immigration bonds. The bond money will return to the fund with interest after the immigration case concludes.
     
  • House know your rights and carefully vetted information about affordable legal services.
     
  • Launch a safe space where communities can support incarcerated immigrants, providing allies from diverse backgrounds a central, coordinated place to take action.
     
  • Share crucial data captured on immigration bond determinations and bond amounts to be used to expose systematic problems regarding immigration bonds.

Michael Santomauro

Michael has advocated for immigration rights for 10 years. He began serving indigent and immigrant communities in 2007 when working in refugee resettlement programs, followed by as stint as a DOJ (then BIA) accredited representative acting for clients before the USCIS. Since completing law school and throughout his career as an advocacy attorney in D.C. and Chicago, he has been committed to working for fair immigration laws and policies.
